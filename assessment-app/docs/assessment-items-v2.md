## SECTION A: ORVIS — Vocational Interest Items (33 items)
**Source:** Oregon Vocational Interest Scales (Pozzebon et al., 2010) — Public Domain
**Format:** "How much would you enjoy doing each of the following activities?" (1 = Very Much Dislike, 2 = Dislike, 3 = Neutral, 4 = Enjoy, 5 = Very Much Enjoy)
**Scoring:** All items are positively keyed. Sum items per scale. Rank scales highest to lowest.
**Function:** Primary archetype signal for Mobilizer, Organizer, Connector, Creator. Partial signal for Analyst, Philosopher, and Builder.

### A1: Leadership → MOBILIZER (5 items selected from 12)
| # | Item |
|---|------|
| A1 | Make important things happen |
| A2 | Lead other people |
| A3 | Persuade others to change their views |
| A4 | Debate topics in a public meeting |
| A5 | Make decisions that affect a lot of people |

*Items removed: "Be a sales or marketing director," "Be the chief executive of a large company," "Organize a political campaign," "Be the master of ceremonies at a meeting," "Plan an advertising campaign," "Be a state governor or senator," "Run for political office." Rationale: Too corporate/political for 17-18 year olds. Retained items capture the Mobilizer's core drive — rallying people, leading, influencing — without narrowing to specific career contexts.*

## SECTION B: CEI-II — Curiosity & Exploration (10 items)

**Format:** "Rate the statements below for how accurately they reflect the way you generally feel and behave." (1 = Very Slightly or Not at All, 2 = A Little, 3 = Moderately, 4 = Quite a Bit, 5 = Extremely)
**Scoring:** Sum all 10 items for total curiosity score. Subscale scores: Stretching (odd items), Embracing (even items).
**Function:** Primary archetype signal for EXPLORER.

## SECTION C: NCS-6 — Need for Cognition (6 items)
**Source:** Need for Cognition Scale-6 (Coelho et al., 2018; based on Cacioppo & Petty, 1982) — Free for research use with citation
**Format:** "For each sentence below, please select how characteristic this is for you personally." (1 = Extremely Uncharacteristic, 2 = Somewhat Uncharacteristic, 3 = Uncertain, 4 = Somewhat Characteristic, 5 = Extremely Characteristic)
**Scoring:** Reverse score items C3 and C4, then sum all 6. Higher = greater need for cognition.
**Function:** Primary archetype signal for PHILOSOPHER. Secondary signal for ANALYST. Differentiates Thinking-oriented archetypes from others.
**Note:** These items must remain verbatim per author requirements.

| # | Item | Keying |
|---|------|--------|
| C1 | I would prefer complex to simple problems | + |
| C2 | I like to have the responsibility of handling a situation that requires a lot of thinking | + |
| C3 | Thinking is not my idea of fun | − (reverse) |
| C4 | I would rather do something that requires little thought than something that is sure to challenge my thinking abilities | − (reverse) |
| C5 | I really enjoy a task that involves coming up with new solutions to problems | + |
| C6 | I would prefer a task that is intellectual, difficult, and important to one that is somewhat important but does not require much thought | + |

## SECTION E: Dweck Kind of Person Scale — Growth Mindset (8 items)
**Source:** Implicit Person Theory Scale (Dweck, 1999) — Free for educational use
**Format:** "Please indicate how much you agree or disagree with each of the following statements." (1 = Strongly Agree, 2 = Agree, 3 = Mostly Agree, 4 = Mostly Disagree, 5 = Disagree, 6 = Strongly Disagree)
**Scoring:** Entity items (E1-E4): higher score = more growth mindset. Incremental items (E5-E8): reverse score (subtract from 7), then higher = more growth mindset. Average all 8 items. Score above 4.0 = growth mindset. Below 3.0 = fixed mindset. Between = mixed.
**Function:** Does NOT determine archetype. Shapes the tone of output narrative (invitation vs. empathy vs. challenge).
**Note:** These items must remain verbatim per author requirements.

### Entity Items (agreement = fixed mindset)
| # | Item |
|---|------|
| E1 | The kind of person someone is, is something very basic about them and it can't be changed very much |
| E2 | People can do things differently, but the important parts of who they are can't really be changed |
| E3 | Everyone is a certain kind of person and there is not much that can be done to really change that |
| E4 | As much as I hate to admit it, you can't teach an old dog new tricks. People can't really change their deepest attributes |

### Incremental Items (agreement = growth mindset)
| # | Item |
|---|------|
| E5 | People can always substantially change the kind of person they are |
| E6 | Everyone, no matter who they are, can significantly change their basic characteristics |
| E7 | No matter what kind of person someone is, they can always change very much |
| E8 | People can change even the most basic qualities about themselves |

## SCORING ALGORITHM: How Items Map to Archetypes

### Step 1: Calculate Raw Scores

| Signal | Calculation | Max Score |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Mobilizer | Sum(A1–A5) | 25 |
| Organizer | Sum(A6–A10) | 25 |
| Connector | Sum(A11–A15) | 25 |
| Creator | Sum(A16–A20) | 25 |
| Analyst (partial) | Sum(A21–A25) | 25 |
| Philosopher (partial) | Sum(A26–A30) | 25 |
| Builder (partial — Production) | Sum(A31–A33) | 15 |
| Explorer | Sum(B1–B10) | 50 |
| Builder (primary — Achievement/Efficacy) | Sum(D1–D8, with D4 and D8 reverse-scored) | 40 |
| Need for Cognition | Sum(C1–C6, with C3 and C4 reverse-scored) | 30 |
| Growth Mindset | Average of all E items (with E5–E8 reverse-scored) | 6.0 |

### Step 2: Normalize Scores to Common Scale

Because different instruments have different ranges, normalize all archetype signals to a 0–100 scale before comparing:

Normalized Score = ((Raw Score − Minimum Possible) / (Maximum Possible − Minimum Possible)) × 100

### Step 3: Calculate Composite Archetype Scores

| Archetype | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| **Philosopher** | (0.35 × ORVIS Erudition) + (0.40 × NCS-6) + (0.15 × Intellect facet) + (0.10 × Imagination facet) |
| **Explorer** | (0.75 × CEI-II Total) + (0.15 × CEI-II Embracing subscale bonus*) + (0.10 × low Orderliness) |
| **Builder** | (0.20 × ORVIS Production) + (0.40 × Achievement-Striving + Self-Efficacy combined) + (0.20 × low Vulnerability) + (0.15 × Self-Discipline) + (0.05 × low-other-scales discount**) |
| **Organizer** | (0.65 × ORVIS Organization) + (0.25 × Orderliness facet) + (0.10 × Self-Discipline) |
| **Analyst** | (0.30 × ORVIS Analysis) + (0.30 × NCS-6) + (0.20 × Orderliness) + (0.20 × Intellect facet) |
| **Connector** | (0.65 × ORVIS Altruism) + (0.20 × Cooperation) + (0.15 × low Assertiveness) |
| **Mobilizer** | (0.55 × ORVIS Leadership) + (0.30 × Assertiveness facet) + (0.15 × low Cooperation*) |
| **Creator** | (0.55 × ORVIS Creativity) + (0.25 × Artistic Interests facet) + (0.20 × Imagination facet) |

*Embracing bonus: if Embracing subscale > Stretching subscale, add bonus weight (this differentiates Explorer from general curiosity)
*Low Cooperation for Mobilizer: not that low cooperation is required, but higher assertiveness + lower deference = leader who pushes, not just follows

**Builder low-other-scales discount: If the respondent also scores in the top 2 on Mobilizer, Philosopher, or Explorer, apply a discount to the Builder composite. High Achievement-Striving + high ORVIS Leadership = Mobilizer, not Builder. High Achievement-Striving + high NCS-6 = Analyst or Philosopher, not Builder. The Builder's achievement drive should be channeled primarily into making/executing, not into leading, theorizing, or experiencing. Implementation: if max(Mobilizer, Philosopher, Explorer) > Builder before discount, reduce Builder by 10–15%. Exact discount to be calibrated with founding cohort.

**Important: These weights are starting hypotheses. They MUST be refined through testing with real students. The founding cohort is your calibration group.**

### Step 4: Assign Primary and Secondary Archetypes

1. Rank all 8 composite scores highest to lowest.
2. Highest = Primary Archetype.
3. Second highest = Secondary Archetype.
4. If top two scores are within 5 points of each other on the normalized scale, flag as "balanced" profile and present both equally.
5. If top three scores are within 5 points, consider a "Renaissance" profile output — "you resist easy categorization, which is actually a strength."

### Step 5: Determine Output Modifiers

| Modifier | Source | Effect on Output |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| Growth Mindset Score | Section E average | High (>4.0): Lean into challenge and possibility. Low (<3.0): Lean into empathy and gentle invitation. Mixed: Balance both. |
| Gregariousness | D21–D22 | High: Describe archetype in social terms. Low: Describe archetype in independent/internal terms. |
| Vulnerability | D25–D26 | High: Shadow section is more empathetic, less confrontational. Low: Shadow section can be more direct. |
| Self-Consciousness | D27–D28 | High: Emphasize safety and belonging in the invitation. Low: Emphasize adventure and challenge. |
| Self-Discipline | D17–D18 | Low: Include "follow-through" in shadow section if Explorer or Mobilizer. |
| Cooperation | D23–D24 | Low: Include "intensity with others" in shadow section if Analyst or Mobilizer. |

## ITEM PRESENTATION ORDER

Items should NOT be presented grouped by instrument. They should be mixed/randomized to prevent response set bias and keep the experience varied. Recommended approach:

1. Open with 3-5 CEI-II items (engaging, self-reflective — good warm-up)
2. Mix ORVIS items across scales (don't present all Leadership items in a row)
3. Intersperse IPIP-NEO items throughout
4. Place NCS-6 items in the middle third
5. Distribute the 3 new ORVIS Production items across the middle section (don't cluster them)
6. End with Dweck growth mindset items (reflective, philosophical — good closing)

The student should never see section headers or instrument names. They experience one continuous flow of questions.
