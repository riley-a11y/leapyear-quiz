Archetype Card Explorations

Direction I
The Figure
A character for each archetype — someone you recognize in yourself
Each card features a stylized human figure rendered in the mixed-media collage style. Not a portrait of a real person, but a constructed character made from the materials and textures of their archetype. The Organizer is composed of golden grids and architectural blueprints. The Explorer is woven from topographic lines and horizon light. The figure is slightly abstracted — you see yourself in the posture, the gesture, the energy. Not the face.
[Organizer Figure]

Collage character: a figure whose torso dissolves into architectural blueprints, hands resting on a geometric grid. Golden amber light behind. Head slightly turned, looking at something being built. Fragments of graph paper and compass roses layered in. Confident, deliberate posture.

Direction II
The Artifact
Objects that hold meaning — tools, relics, instruments
Instead of a character, each archetype is represented by a still life of meaningful objects. The Organizer gets a brass compass on blueprints. The Creator gets pigment jars and a half-finished canvas. The card feels like you've been given something — a relic from someone who came before you, passed down for the mission ahead. Museum-placard typography, clean framing, the object is the star.
[Organizer Artifact]

Still life: A brass drafting compass resting on unfolded architectural blueprints. A worn leather notebook nearby, open to a page of precise hand-drawn grids. Golden light from left. Clean dark background. The objects feel old and valuable. Mixed-media: real photography with subtle geometric overlay and graph-paper texture bleeding in at edges.

Direction III
The Landscape
Every archetype has a world — step into yours
Each card is a full-bleed scene. Not where you live, but where your soul lives. The Organizer's landscape is a golden-hour cityscape seen through a geometric grid, where order meets beauty. The Explorer's is a vast desert horizon with a single road vanishing into orange distance. The Philosopher's is a library carved into a cliff face, lit by deep blue twilight. The card is a window. You look through it and recognize something.
[Organizer Landscape]

A golden-hour cityscape seen from above — orderly streets radiating from a central point, geometric buildings glowing in amber sunset light. A subtle architectural grid overlay, like looking through drafting paper. Warm amber and gold tones dominating. Not a specific city, but the platonic ideal of one. Shot with a slight tilt-shift blur. Mixed-media: photograph with cartographic grid lines and topographic contours overlaid.
